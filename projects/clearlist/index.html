Clear the List: Spending the Last $10K Where It Mattered Most

At the end of the fiscal year, with back-to-school season underway, we had $10,000 left in the ad budget and a choice to make. We chose to put it directly into Michigan classrooms — and let that action do the marketing.

What
Michigan educators submitted classroom Amazon wish lists. We selected 19 and funded them completely.
Why
Authenticity is a strategy. Proving we cared about teachers was worth more than one more awareness campaign.

The Situation

Teachers across the country routinely spend their own money on classroom supplies — an average of $750 out of pocket per year, a number that had climbed higher in the wake of pandemic-era inflation. Many post Amazon wish lists hoping strangers will help fill the gaps.

At Michigan Virtual, we had spent two decades supporting teachers through professional development. But back-to-school season 2022 felt like a moment to do something more direct. We had $10,000 remaining in the advertising budget at the end of our fiscal year. The question was what to do with it.

The Idea

Instead of running one more awareness campaign, I proposed we use that budget to fund Michigan teachers' classroom wish lists directly. We invited educators statewide to submit their lists, selected 19 winners based on school need, submission message, and alignment with Michigan Virtual's mission — then purchased the supplies and shipped them to classrooms across the state.

The funded classrooms represented the full breadth of Michigan education:

  • A hands-on science lab for a classroom in Manistique
  • A Detroit classroom lending library centered on diverse, culturally relatable books
  • Games and learning tools for a special education classroom in Homer
  • Basic necessities for a Grand Rapids classroom where 10% of students were experiencing homelessness
  • Two new iPads for an Upper Peninsula classroom
  • Charging stations for students in Muskegon who couldn't charge devices at home
  • Spanish language materials for 8th graders in Kalamazoo

The Pushback — and Why It Was Worth Navigating

Not everyone was immediately on board. The internal concern was that spending state-adjacent budget on teacher wish lists might look like we had too much money to burn — that it could invite scrutiny rather than goodwill.

My read was the opposite. Michigan Virtual exists to serve Michigan's K-12 community. Teachers are our most important audience. Proving that we understood their daily reality — not just their enrollment decisions — was exactly the kind of signal that builds durable trust. An awareness campaign buys attention for a week. This built something harder to manufacture: credibility that lasts.

What Happened

The campaign generated significant organic reach and genuine community response. Over 1,100 educators engaged statewide — far exceeding expectations. Teachers shared their lists, colleagues tagged one another, and the story spread well beyond Michigan Virtual's owned channels.

In the Press

The campaign was picked up by Fox 17 News, extending reach to a statewide audience with no additional media spend.

All submitted wish lists remained public on the campaign page throughout the school year, and Michigan Virtual encouraged the broader community to keep contributing to their local teachers — extending the campaign's life and goodwill well beyond the original $10,000.

It became one of the most talked-about campaigns we ran that year — not measured in click-through rates, but in something harder to manufacture: people feeling like Michigan Virtual was genuinely on their side.
