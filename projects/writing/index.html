Why I Don’t Give (Hear Me Out)

Guest article on donor psychology and relationship-based fundraising — ranked #3 most-read by a newsletter for thousands of nonprofit professionals.

At a Glance

What

A guest essay examining how frequent, guilt-driven fundraising appeals can erode trust — and offering practical strategies to build durable donor relationships through empathy, transparency, and meaningful non-monetary engagement.

Why

Written to champion the donor experience in nonprofit communications. Drawing on professional marketing expertise and personal context, the piece advocates for fewer pressure tactics and more authentic, trust-based interactions.

Impact

Published on Fundraising for Breakfast (by the VP of Fundraising at the United Way of Central Maryland); reached thousands of fundraisers and rose to the site’s #3 most-read article.

Want the full story and recommendations?

Includes tactics for pacing asks, balancing guilt with grace, and offering other ways to engage.

Excerpt

A few highlights that capture the tone and takeaway:

“I’m a good tipper. I really am! But not when I feel guilted into doing it… In my recent experience, guilt is starting to feel like it’s become a go-to fundraising tactic. But is it effective?”
“I’m not against giving. I want to help… But like tipping at Qdoba, I hate feeling pressured into it. Because I’m not just your donor. I’m your cause.”
“What I need is a relationship, not a drip campaign… Sometimes the most valuable support doesn’t come with a dollar sign… The most meaningful giving doesn’t come from being made to feel bad. It comes from feeling seen.”

Excerpted for brevity; the full piece includes specific recommendations for pacing asks, balancing guilt with grace, and offering other ways to engage.
