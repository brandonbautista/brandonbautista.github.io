Writing Sample: Why I Don’t Give (Hear Me Out)

Guest post on donor psychology & relationship-based fundraising.

At a Glance

What

A guest essay exploring why high-frequency, guilt-based fundraising nudges can backfire — and why empathy, transparency, and non-monetary engagement build stronger donor relationships.

Why

I wrote this to advocate for customer (donor) experience in the nonprofit space: fewer pressure tactics, more trust and value. It’s grounded in personal lived context and a marketer’s lens on sustainable growth.

Impact

Published on a Substack newsletter by the VP of Fundraising (United Way of Central Maryland), read by thousands of fundraisers; currently #3 most popular article across the site.

Excerpt

“I’m a good tipper. I really am! But not when I feel guilted into doing it… In my recent experience, guilt is starting to feel like it’s become a go-to fundraising tactic. But is it effective?”

“I’m not against giving. I want to help… But like tipping at Qdoba, I hate feeling pressured into it. Because I’m not just your donor. I’m your cause.”

“What I need is a relationship, not a drip campaign… Sometimes the most valuable support doesn’t come with a dollar sign… The most meaningful giving doesn’t come from being made to feel bad. It comes from feeling seen.”

Excerpted for brevity; full context and recommendations in the article.

Context

This piece was invited as a guest post for Fundraising for Breakfast, a Substack by the Vice President of Fundraising at the United Way of Central Maryland, reaching thousands of practitioners. The article blends personal experience with practical guidance for pacing asks, balancing guilt with grace, being transparent about where dollars go, and offering non-monetary ways to engage.
