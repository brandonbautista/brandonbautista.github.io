Who I Am

Putting
people first.

Marketing is a people business dressed up in data.

For 10 years I built campaigns for a public-sector nonprofit — the kind of org where you earn attention rather than buy it, and where the relationship with your audience matters more than any single conversion. That context shaped everything about how I work. I lead with curiosity about the person on the other end. I try to make colleagues feel heard before I push for a decision. I believe the best creative work comes out of environments where people feel safe enough to say the weird idea out loud.

The strategies, the systems, the numbers are real and I'm proud of them. But the thing I care most about is leaving people better than I found them. Audiences, teammates, organizations.
