BRIGHT: Stories of Hope & Innovation in Michigan Classrooms

Award-winning podcast highlighting educators who innovate — building goodwill, not pitching.

Watch on YouTube → Listen on Apple Podcasts →

Project Snapshot

Role

Executive Producer & Marketing Lead

Org/Client

Michigan Virtual

Timeline

Launched YEAR — Ongoing

Tools

HubSpot, YouTube Studio, Premiere Pro, Descript, Spotify for Podcasters

Summary

To provide thought leadership and genuine value to Michigan educators, we launched BRIGHT, a podcast that spotlights teachers doing innovative work in their classrooms — whether or not it involves Michigan Virtual.

Guided by inbound marketing principles, the intent was to create affinity and trust through storytelling rather than selling. Episodes focus on practical ideas, human stories, and pedagogical best practices.

Recognition & Reach: Winner, Best Educational Podcast (37th Annual Educational Advertising Awards). 217,000+ listens across YouTube and podcast platforms.

Objectives & Audience

Objectives

  • Deliver non-promotional content that builds goodwill and brand affinity.
  • Highlight authentic classroom innovation to inspire peers.
  • Strengthen Michigan Virtual’s role as a trusted resource in the educator community.

Primary Audience

  • Michigan educators exploring technology and pedagogy.
  • Leaders seeking real-world examples and repeatable practices.

Strategy & Process

Insight: Educators value peer stories and practical takeaways more than polished product pitches. We centered the teacher’s voice and classroom context.

Strategy: Narrative-first, platform-agnostic distribution (YouTube + podcast feeds), consistent cadence, and guest selection that reflects diverse subjects and grade levels.

Execution: Interview prep and scripting, recording and post-production, HubSpot-powered promotion and nurture, social snippets, and episodic show notes optimized for search and shareability.

Reflection

Centering the teacher’s voice — not the brand — proved that advocacy and storytelling can drive stronger, longer-lasting relationships than direct promotion. If iterating, I’d expand educator co-creation in planning and add short, classroom-ready resource links to each episode.
